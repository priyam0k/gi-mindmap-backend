**Definition Recap**: Committed Capital is a contractual arrangement where an investor agrees to provide capital to an insurer *after* a specific adverse event has occurred that causes the insurer financial distress \[SP7.pdf, 4667, SP8.pdf, 4697, SP7 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf, 4667\]. The insurer effectively purchases an option to issue its securities (e.g., shares) at a predetermined price if a defined trigger event occurs \[SP7.pdf, 4667, SP8.pdf, 4697, SP7 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf, 4667\].

---

The primary advantage of this mechanism is that it serves as a **cost-effective method of protecting the capital base** of an insurance company \[SP7.pdf, 4668, SP8.pdf, 4698, SP7 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf, 4668\]. This core advantage can be broken down into several related benefits:

###### **1\. Solves the Problem of Post-Loss Capital Raising**

* **The Core Problem**: In the immediate aftermath of a major loss event, such as a large catastrophe, an insurer's financial position is weakened \[SP7.pdf, 4667, SP7 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf, 4667, 3655, SP8 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf\]. Consequently, its share price would typically fall, making it very expensive and difficult to raise new capital from the open market precisely when it is most needed \[SP7.pdf, 4667, SP7 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf, 4667\].  
* **The Advantage**: Committed capital provides a direct solution by pre-arranging post-loss financing. The insurer locks in the right to issue securities at a **predetermined price**, which is understood to be much higher than the price that would be available in the market after such an adverse event \[SP7.pdf, 4667, SP8.pdf, 4697, SP7 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf, 4667\]. This ensures capital can be raised at a more favourable rate, protecting existing shareholders from excessive dilution \[SP7.pdf, 4668, SP7 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf, 4668, 3659, SP8 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf\].

###### **2\. Provides Capital Exactly When Needed**

* The arrangement provides a mechanism to ensure that capital is provided as it is required, specifically **following a deterioration of experience** \[SP7.pdf, 4668, SP8.pdf, 4698, SP7 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf, 4668\]. The use of one or more "triggers" in the contract ensures the option can only be exercised in genuine cases of financial distress, which mitigates moral hazard and makes the product viable for investors \[SP7.pdf, 4668, SP8.pdf, 4698, SP7 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf, 4668\].

###### **3\. Capital Efficiency and Cost-Effectiveness**

* Committed capital is considered a **cost-effective** way to protect an insurer's capital base \[SP7.pdf, 4668, SP8.pdf, 4698, SP7 CMP Upgrade 2022.pdf, 4668\]. Instead of holding a large amount of physical capital on the balance sheet at all times (which has an opportunity cost), the insurer pays a smaller fee (the option premium) to have the right to call on capital when a specific contingency occurs. This frees up capital for other uses within the business.
